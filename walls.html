what people leave behind

abandoned places are crime scenes of ordinary life. when people leave in a hurry — or are taken — the room stays mid-sentence.

  • medicine still in the cabinet, expired by decades.
  • a child's shoe. singular. no explanation.
  • polaroids of people who don't know they're on a stranger's wall now.
  • letters that were never sent.
  • a hospital wing sealed since a patient disappeared and no one updated the paperwork.
  • handwriting on the inside of a closet door. low down. child-height.

objects outlive context. that's the horror of it.

forensics of a room

you can read a space like a body. every surface has a story if you know what to look for.

  • dust settles at ~0.1mm per year on undisturbed surfaces. you can date a room.
  • lividity. pooling. distribution. rooms have them too.
  • wear patterns on floors tell you where someone stood most often. habitual paths. worry paths.
  • mold growth indicates moisture. moisture indicates warmth. recent warmth.
  • the smell of copper is not rust.
"every locked door is a question someone decided not to answer."
between the walls, literally

in forensics, in history, in the places people don't check — the walls themselves hold things.

  • in 1991, a sealed room in a paris apartment was opened after 70 years. the tenant had fled the nazi occupation. everything was still there.
  • contractors regularly find compartments, letters, coins, and remains during renovations.
  • the practice of walling something in — structurally or socially — is older than recorded history.
  • some things are hidden deliberately. some things are just forgotten. the room doesn't distinguish.
