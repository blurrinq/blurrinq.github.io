how to disappear

a practical, incomplete guide.

// this is not a metaphor.
somewhere. you don't need to know where.
digital

the internet remembers everything you were too careless to hide. start there.

  • use a VPN that does not log. not one that says it doesn't — one that structurally cannot.
  • stop using your real name for anything that doesn't legally require it.
  • email is not private. it was never private. treat it like a postcard.
  • your phone is a tracking device that also makes calls. act accordingly.
  • browser fingerprinting doesn't need cookies. your screen resolution, fonts, and timezone are enough.
  • delete old accounts. they're attack surface, nothing more.
  • encrypted DNS. always. your ISP sees every domain you query otherwise.
  • the metadata of a file tells more than its contents. strip it.
physical

most people are findable because they're predictable. routine is a vulnerability.

  • pay cash when it matters.
  • CCTV coverage in most cities is over 80% of public space. walk like you know that.
  • EXIF data in photos contains GPS coordinates. your camera is confessing.
  • loyalty cards sell your purchase history. the discount is not free.
  • people remember faces, not ████████████████.
  • the best time to change your pattern is before you need to.
the city doesn't notice you. that's the point.
identity

the hardest part isn't the technical side. it's the social graph. you can encrypt everything and still be findable through the people who know you.

  • compartmentalize. different people know different versions of you.
  • what you don't post is more powerful than what you do.
  • your interests, location, schedule, and habits — together — are a fingerprint.
  • silence is underrated.
"the most dangerous person is the one no one is looking for."
why bother

not paranoia. just the logical conclusion of paying attention.

you don't have to be hiding from anything specific to value not being seen. privacy is not guilt. it's architecture.

disappearing isn't about becoming no one. it's about choosing who gets to know ████ ███ ████.
